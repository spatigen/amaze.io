Amaze Bench supports four maze shapes (circle, hexagon, square, triangle) with sizes ranging from 3×3 to 16×16.

What is Amaze?

Image editing models are great at seeing, but can they plan? This repository introduces Amaze, a benchmark designed to evaluate image editing models on visual planning tasks.

Given a maze layout, we ask the model to produce an edited image that solves or reflects the structure. Unlike traditional evaluations on image generating, we use automatic metrics to precisely evaluate the performance of image editing models.

Iterative Visual Planning

You can use different models (like Bagel, Janus-Pro-7B, Qwen-Image-Edit) to solve mazes step-by-step. The model acts as a visual planner, refining the path in latent space.

Evaluation Metrics

Comprehensive assessment logic in infer/maze_metrics.py:

  • MSE: Pixel-level structural fidelity.
  • Solvability: Conceptual-level accuracy.
  • Robustness: Tested across 4 shapes & variable sizes.
